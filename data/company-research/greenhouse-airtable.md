# Airtable — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:airtable | company_type: product

What they do: Airtable is a no-code / low-code collaborative database and workflow automation platform. It blends spreadsheet familiarity with relational database power, letting non-technical users build apps, track projects, and automate workflows. It has pivoted increasingly toward AI-driven automation, launching "Superagent" (standalone product, Jan 2026) and acquiring DeepSky (Oct 2025, AI team). Enterprise customers use it for operational databases, project tracking, CRM, and content pipelines.

How they describe themselves: Positioned as the "connected apps platform" empowering teams to build their own workflows. Emphasizes democratizing software building for business teams. Engineering culture values technical depth — infrastructure and compute engineering roles require 4–8 or 8+ years of experience and target senior IC talent. The company acquired an AI team (DeepSky, 3 co-founders + 12 engineers) in 2025, signaling an AI-first engineering direction.

Size / stage / funding: ~937 employees (Apr 2026), down from a peak of ~1,500 (significant headcount reductions in 2023–2024). Founded 2013. Series F. Total funding: ~$1.35B from 59 investors; largest round $735M Series F in Dec 2021. Valuation ~$11B at peak (2021), since written down to ~$4–5B. HQ: San Francisco, CA. Offices in Austin TX, New York NY, London UK. Remote hiring in Seattle WA and Los Angeles CA.

Locations / HQ: San Francisco CA (HQ), New York NY, Austin TX, London UK. Remote hires in select US cities (Seattle, LA) for some teams.

Remote policy: Hybrid for SF/NY/Austin/London employees. Remote-US only for select teams and roles. Engineering roles listed as "Remote - US" or location-restricted to SF/NY/Seattle. No remote-Canada roles found in current listings.

No mention of Canada in any active engineering job posting. The company does not appear to have a Canadian entity or remote-Canada hiring program.

Engineering & tech: Significant platform engineering — Compute team (distributed computation, serverless functions, multi-tenant runtime), Infrastructure team (reliability, scalability, developer tooling), Data team, and Product Backend. Stack: inferred as Node.js / TypeScript on backend, React frontend, PostgreSQL, AWS. Scale challenges around multi-tenant SaaS performance and real-time collaboration. The Compute role (8+ YOE) signals a dedicated compute/serverless platform investment.

Notable / other: Significant company reset after 2021 peak — laid off 27% in 2023, further reductions since. Now at ~937 employees. AI pivot (Superagent product, DeepSky acquisition) represents a strategic bet on agentic workflows. Despite the reset, it remains a well-capitalized private company with substantial enterprise contracts. $4B current valuation estimate. Engineering roles at 8+ YOE level suggest a more senior-heavy org post-cuts.
