# Slack — research
Provider/key: workday:salesforce/slack | company_type: product

What they do: Slack is a business messaging and collaboration platform, owned by Salesforce since 2021 ($27.7B acquisition). It is one of the most widely used enterprise communication tools globally, with deep integrations across the Salesforce ecosystem. Slack is currently being positioned as an AI-native work platform through the launch of Slackbot (general availability 2026), embedding agents and AI automation directly into the messaging layer.

How they describe themselves: "We believe in your freedom to work when and how you work best, to help us all thrive." Core values: Empathy, Courtesy, Thriving, Craftsmanship, Playfulness, Solidarity. Engineering culture emphasizes scale and reliability — Slack handles billions of messages and maintains multi-region infrastructure. The Data Engineering team describes itself as "the engine behind every great enterprise decision," building batch and real-time pipelines for the largest enterprise customers.

Size / stage / funding: Part of Salesforce (NYSE: CRM), a $30B+ revenue public company. Slack itself has thousands of employees within the Salesforce org. Slack HQ: San Francisco, CA. Part of a company with offices in 30+ cities globally.

Locations / HQ: San Francisco CA (Slack HQ), Seattle WA, Atlanta GA, Bellevue WA, New York NY, Denver CO, and other US cities. Salesforce has a Berlin office (one Slack infrastructure role listed there). No dedicated Canada office for Slack specifically, though Salesforce has a Canadian presence (Toronto).

Remote policy: Mixed/Salesforce hybrid. Salesforce's 2026 model: "Office-Focused" (4 days/week) for Sales/Global Tech/Product, "Office-Flex" (3 days/week or 10 days/quarter), or "Remote" (primarily home/customer site). Engineering roles appear to have some remote flexibility but specific Slack engineering roles advertised show only US cities (SF, Seattle, Atlanta, Bellevue, Dallas). Some roles list "remote" with travel, primarily US-anchored.

Remote-Canada eligibility: Unclear/unlikely for most roles. The careers.salesforce.com page shows Slack engineering roles restricted to US locations (SF, Seattle, Atlanta, Bellevue, Dallas). One older job aggregator reference noted that "remote positions open to employees in Canada" and that the Cloud Network team spans "Australia, US, and Canada," but no current posting confirms this. A Berlin-based infrastructure role suggests some international flexibility. In practice, Salesforce has a Canadian entity but Slack-specific engineering roles appear to be US-centric. Quebec roles would require bilingual French/English.

Engineering & tech: Scale infrastructure for a massive enterprise messaging system. Stack includes Java, Go, PHP/Hack, Python, MySQL, Solr, Kubernetes, Terraform, AWS, Linux. Teams: Cloud Infrastructure, Service Network, Data Infrastructure, ML Infrastructure, Data Ingestion. The Cloud Infrastructure and Data Infrastructure teams handle real-time and batch pipeline work at significant scale.

Notable / other: Slack was acquired by Salesforce in 2021. The Slackbot AI agent (Agentforce integration) launched in 2026. Under Salesforce, the engineering culture has become more corporate compared to the pre-acquisition startup era. Salesforce is investing heavily in physical offices (305,000 sq ft campus in Sunnyvale). Some engineering talent has moved to competing products (Discord, Teams, etc.) since acquisition.

Open relevant roles (sample): Sr. Data Engineer, Enterprise - Slack; Software Engineer (Multiple Levels) - ML Infrastructure; Software Engineer I/II, Service Network; Software Engineer II/Sr., Cloud Infrastructure; Staff Software Engineer, Data Ingestion; Staff Software Engineer, Data Infrastructure.
