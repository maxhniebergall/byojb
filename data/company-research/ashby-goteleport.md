# Teleport — research
Provider/key: ashby:goteleport | company_type: product

What they do: Teleport is "the Infrastructure Identity Company," providing a unified platform for identity-based access control across infrastructure — servers, Kubernetes clusters, databases, applications, and cloud resources. Their product enforces zero-trust access, privileged access management, and policy governance for engineering teams. Customers include Nasdaq, Discord, Airtable, dbt Labs, and Elastic.

How they describe themselves: "Making infrastructure trustworthy" is their stated mission. They describe their product as "modernizing identity, access, and policy for infrastructure" and "trusted computing" that "helps engineering teams accelerate development while improving infrastructure security." Core values/behaviors: "We write it down" (docs-first), "We are professionals," "We start simple and iterate," "We are drivers, not passengers," "We are builders," "We lift each other up," "We assume the principle of charity," and "We value each other's time." Culture tone: senior/high-ownership, async-friendly, no-passenger-riders philosophy. They describe themselves as "a senior team" that accepts only "top talent." Recognition: Inc. 5000, Fast Company Most Innovative 2024, Global Info Sec Awards 2026, Fortune Cyber 60 List 2026.

Size / stage / funding: Raised $110M Series C at a $1.1B valuation (unicorn). Backed by Bessemer Venture Partners, Insight Partners, Kleiner Perkins, Y Combinator, S28 Capital. 600+ customers globally. Headcount not publicly disclosed but estimated in the low hundreds.

Locations / HQ: Headquarters in Oakland, CA (2100 Franklin St). Additional offices in Seattle and Toronto. Significant distributed/remote workforce globally.

Remote policy: Remote-first with global distribution. Benefits include home office stipend and internet/phone allowance. Employees noted on Glassdoor that the culture supports async and remote-friendly work. One source cited remote Backend Software Engineer roles available "anywhere in Canada, Europe or the United States."

Remote-Canada eligibility: YES — verified. Teleport has a Toronto office and explicitly hires remote engineers in Canada. Job postings on aggregators (RemoteOK, NoDesk) confirm Canada-eligible backend engineering roles.

Engineering & tech: Core product is written primarily in Go and Rust. Focuses on distributed systems security, identity protocols (mTLS, SPIFFE/SPIRE, SSH certificates, OIDC/SAML), Kubernetes, cloud infrastructure. Engineering organized around product pillars: Security, Platform Security, Machine & Workload Identity, Infrastructure Access. Open source component (gravitational/teleport on GitHub) with enterprise edition.

Notable / other: Open-source roots; the core product is available on GitHub. Active in the developer security/zero-trust market (growing rapidly post-SolarWinds, post-Okta incidents). Won multiple security industry awards in 2025–2026. Named to Fortune Cyber 60 in 2026.

Open relevant roles (sample): Senior Software Engineer - Security, Senior Backend Engineer, Senior Backend Engineer - Platform Security, Senior Backend Engineer - Machine & Workload Identity
