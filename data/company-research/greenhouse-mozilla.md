# Mozilla — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:mozilla | company_type: product
What they do: Mozilla develops open-source software, most notably the Firefox web browser, alongside privacy-centric consumer products (like Mozilla VPN, Relay) and open AI research (Mozilla.ai).
How they describe themselves: Guided by the "Mozilla Manifesto," which declares that the internet is a global public resource that must remain open, accessible, and user-centric. They prioritize user privacy, security, and digital sovereignty. Their tone is mission-driven, community-focused, and collaborative.
Size / stage / funding: Established, private/non-profit hybrid structure (Mozilla Foundation and Mozilla Corporation), ~1,000+ employees.
Locations / HQ: Mountain View, California (HQ) with global hubs.
Remote policy: Remote-first with a highly distributed team. They emphasize asynchronous work and healthy work-life balance.
Remote-Canada eligibility: Verified yes. They actively hire and support fully remote roles across Canada, providing a comprehensive benefits package (including home office stipends).
Engineering & tech: High focus on systems programming, web engine development (Gecko, Rust components), data ingestion pipelines, telemetry, and secure AI infrastructure. Tech stack includes Rust, C++, Python, JavaScript/TypeScript, Go, and AWS/GCP cloud platforms.
Notable / other: Prominent pioneer of Rust and open-source web standards. They host the popular hacks.mozilla.org blog for deep technical web and browser architecture content.
Open relevant roles (sample): Senior Backend Engineer, Telemetry Data Pipelines; Systems Engineer, Firefox Security; Senior Release Engineer.
Sources: mozilla.org, mozilla.org/careers, hacks.mozilla.org.
