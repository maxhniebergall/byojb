# Snowflake — research
Provider/key: ashby:snowflake | company_type: product
What they do: Snowflake develops a cloud-based data storage and analytics service, generally termed "data warehouse-as-a-service." They offer a unified platform for data engineering, data lake storage, data sharing, and AI/ML workloads (the Snowflake AI Data Cloud).
How they describe themselves: Driven by the mission to "enable every organization to be data-driven." Their culture revolves around five core values: "Put Customers First," "Integrity Always," "Think Big," "Be Excellent," and "Make Each Other the Best." They cultivate an engineering-focused, collaborative environment centered on high reliability.
Size / stage / funding: Public company (NYSE: SNOW), established enterprise, ~7,000+ employees.
Locations / HQ: Bozeman, Montana (HQ) with a major engineering hub in Toronto, Ontario.
Remote policy: Hybrid or office-based for most engineering roles, particularly in Canada.
Remote-Canada eligibility: Mixed/Limited. While they employ in Canada, software engineering roles are generally tied to their Toronto office and require in-office or hybrid presence, rather than allowing full remote work from anywhere in Canada.
Engineering & tech: Deep focus on SQL query compilation, distributed databases, cloud resource optimization, and ML platform engineering. Technology stack includes Java, C++, Go, Python, and SQL on top of AWS, GCP, and Azure.
Notable / other: Maintains an active Medium publication (Snowflake Builders) and an engineering blog covering high-scale database query architectures and sovereign AI.
Open relevant roles (sample): Senior Data Platform Engineer; Senior Distributed Systems Engineer.
Sources: careers.snowflake.com, snowflake.com/blog.
