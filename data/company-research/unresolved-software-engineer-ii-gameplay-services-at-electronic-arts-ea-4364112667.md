# Electronic Arts (EA) — research
Provider/key: unresolved:software-engineer-ii-gameplay-services-at-electronic-arts-ea-4364112667 | company_type: product
What they do: Electronic Arts (EA) is a leading global digital interactive entertainment publisher. They develop and deliver games, content, and online services for Internet-connected consoles, mobile devices, and personal computers (e.g., EA SPORTS, Battlefield, Apex Legends).
How they describe themselves: They describe themselves as a creative company that inspires the world to play. They highlight values such as passion, pioneering, teamwork, determination, and learning.
Size / stage / funding: Public company (NASDAQ: EA). Headcount is approximately 13,000+ employees globally.
Locations / HQ: Redwood City, California (HQ). In Canada, they have a major development studio in Vancouver (Burnaby), BC.
Remote policy: Hybrid model. EA enforces a policy requiring employees living within 30 miles (48 km) of an office to work in-office at least 3 days per week. "Offsite local" remote models have been phased out, and fully remote arrangements require senior leadership approval.
Remote-Canada eligibility: Verified No / highly restricted. Due to their shift back to office and RTO mandates, remote hiring is extremely limited and requires executive exception.
Engineering & tech: C++, Java, Kotlin, gameplay server services, multiplayer infrastructure, cloud hosting, game engines (Frostbite).
Notable / other: The Vancouver studio is one of the largest game development hubs in the world.
Open relevant roles (sample): Software Engineer II - Gameplay Services.
