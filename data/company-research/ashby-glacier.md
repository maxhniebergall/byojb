# Glacier — research
Provider/key: ashby:glacier | company_type: product

What they do: Glacier is an AI robotics company that automates recycling and material recovery. They build compact robotic sorter arms equipped with AI computer vision that can identify and sort 70+ types of recyclable materials at material recovery facilities (MRFs). Their second product is a real-time data dashboard for brands to get item-level visibility into what recyclable materials are flowing through the waste stream. Mission: "End waste by ensuring nothing valuable ends up in a landfill."

How they describe themselves: "Next-generation recycling technology." Glacier positions itself as a mission-driven company building "for real material recovery facilities, with real operators, real throughput demands, real maintenance constraints, and real financial pressure to make every line work harder." Values center on sustainability, impact, and technical rigor. Named Fast Company's #1 Most Innovative Company in Robotics & Engineering in 2026.

Size / stage / funding: Series A. Total funding ~$28.2M over 3 rounds from 20 investors. Most recent: $16M Series A (April 2025), Amazon-backed. ~12 employees as of mid-2025, with plans to expand to ~55 full-time employees. Founders: Areeb Malik and Rebecca Hu Thrams. Based in San Francisco.

Locations / HQ: San Francisco, CA (HQ). Engineering team is distributed; roles explicitly targeting Latin America for remote positions. No Canadian presence identified.

Remote policy: Roles are remote but specifically targeted at Latin America for engineering positions ("Fully Remote USD — Latin America"). The "Fully Remote USD" compensation structure and Latin America geographic restriction are explicit in all engineering job postings reviewed.

Remote-Canada eligibility: No. All engineering remote roles explicitly state "Latin America" as the target region. The Data Platform roles list "Fully Remote USD — Latin America" in their titles and descriptions. Canada is not included.

Engineering & tech: Computer vision AI (trained on billions of recycling images), data platform engineering (pipelines, dashboards, brand analytics), DevOps/infrastructure, robotics software. Stack inferred: Python (ML/CV), data pipeline tooling, cloud infrastructure (AWS/GCP). Data Platform roles involve building data pipelines and analytics infrastructure for recycling material tracking.

Notable / other: Strong mission/impact story; Amazon partnership; Fast Company recognition. Very early-stage (~12 employees currently). The Latin America-only remote engineering model suggests a cost-arbitrage hiring strategy. The Data Platform roles are the relevant ones for backend/data engineering backgrounds, but the geographic restriction is a hard disqualifier for Canada-based candidates.
