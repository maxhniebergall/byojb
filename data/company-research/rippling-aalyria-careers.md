# Aalyria — research
Provider/key: rippling:aalyria-careers | company_type: product

What they do: Aalyria develops networking technology for high-throughput, mobile communications systems — primarily for space and aerial environments. Their two main products are: (1) Spacetime, a network orchestration platform (PaaS) for managing networks in motion (satellite, aerial, terrestrial); and (2) Tightbeam, a free-space optical (laser) communication terminal delivering up to 100 Gbps over extreme distances. They serve government (US Air Force, Space Force, AFRL, NRL) and commercial satellite operators.

How they describe themselves: "Directivity x Mobility" — enabling "commercial and government customers to operate resilient, high-throughput networks in motion." Mission is to build the next-generation communications backbone for the New Space Age (per Series B announcement, Feb 2026).

Size / stage / funding: $135M total raised. Series B: $100M closed February 2026, led by Battery Ventures and J2 Ventures. Post-money valuation: $1.3B (unicorn). Backed by Google/Alphabet (retaining stake). Founded 2021 as a Google spinout. ~100-200 employees estimated. HQ in Livermore, CA.

Locations / HQ: HQ in Livermore, CA. Also Mountain View, CA; Washington DC area; and London UK (3 UK positions). 18 total open roles as of batch research date.

Remote policy: Mixed. Out of 18 open roles: 9 are "Remote (US)," 3 are hybrid, 6 are on-site specific. Remote roles appear to be US-only remote. One Canada-based role found: a "Mission Engineer" position in Canada under the Spacetime division. Engineering backend roles (Backend Software Engineer, Senior Backend Software Engineer, Senior Software Engineer Platforms) are listed as "Remote (US)."

Remote-Canada eligibility: No for most engineering roles — US-only remote. One Canada position exists (Mission Engineer, Spacetime) but it is not a software engineering/backend/platform role. The backend and platform engineer roles specifically state "United States Remote." Not Canada-eligible for the roles relevant to this candidate.

Engineering & tech: Spacetime: network orchestration software, real-time optimization for dynamic topologies, 5G-NTN integration, satellite mesh networking. Tightbeam: optical terminal hardware + control software. Backend engineering uses Go and/or Python (typical for aerospace SaaS). DTN (Delay/Disruption Tolerant Networking) protocol stack. Platform work involves cloud infrastructure, distributed software systems. UK-specific roles reference "DTN Software Engineer" and "Machine Learning Engineer."

Notable / other: Spinout from Google's Project Loon and other internal projects. Strong government contracts as primary revenue base. $100M Series B in Feb 2026 for accelerating hiring. UK entity (Spacetime UK) exists as a separate legal entity for UK roles. Company is at an interesting inflection — well-funded, defense/space focus, technically sophisticated.

Open relevant roles (sample): Backend Software Engineer, DTN Software Engineer - Spacetime UK, Machine Learning Engineer - Spacetime UK, Senior Backend Software Engineer, Senior Software Engineer Platforms
