# Vercel — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:vercel | company_type: product

What they do: Vercel is a cloud platform and developer tooling company providing deployment infrastructure, frontend hosting, CDN, AI Gateway, and CI/CD pipelines. They created and maintain Next.js (the dominant React framework), Turbopack, and SWC. Their platform is used by millions of developers to deploy web applications. Competitors include Netlify, Cloudflare Pages, AWS Amplify.

How they describe themselves: "Ship what's Next." Four pillars: (1) Ship-Fast Mentality — "features go from idea to production in days, not quarters"; (2) Open-Source DNA — Next.js, Turbopack, and SWC are publicly maintained; (3) Async-First Collaboration — "pull requests are the primary unit of collaboration"; (4) High Autonomy — engineers have significant freedom with high accountability. Culture rated 4.1/5.0 on satisfaction but work-life balance scores 3.6/5.0, indicating the fast pace creates sustainability tensions.

Size / stage / funding: ~900 employees (mid-2026, up from ~600 a year prior). Series F valuation $9.3B (September 2025). HQ: San Francisco, CA.

Locations / HQ: San Francisco, CA. Other offices: NYC, Austin, London, Berlin, Australia, India, Japan.

Remote policy: Remote-first. Distributed teams across time zones. Async collaboration via GitHub, written documentation, minimal synchronous meetings. Most engineering roles available remotely. No mandatory office attendance.

Remote-Canada eligibility: Likely yes, with caveats. ZipRecruiter lists 18 Vercel job openings in Ontario. Vercel's remote-first model and global distribution suggest Canada eligibility, but the Greenhouse jobs board explicitly lists roles as "Remote - United States" or "Remote - United Kingdom, Germany" — no "Remote - Canada" listed as of latest check. Canada eligibility appears to exist for some roles but is not explicitly confirmed across all engineering positions.

Engineering & tech: Next.js, CDN infrastructure, Edge networking, Compute, AI Gateway, AI SDK, Trust & Safety, Backend systems. Stack heavily TypeScript/Node.js for application layer; Go/Rust likely for edge/CDN infrastructure. Engineering pace described as high, with weekly releases.

Notable / other: Valuation at $9.3B reflects premium placed on developer tools space. Open-source presence very high (Next.js millions of users). Fast-paced culture may not suit "low-chaos" preference. High autonomy but also high velocity expectations.

Open relevant roles (sample): Software Engineer, Backend; Software Engineer, CDN; Software Engineer, CDN Security; Software Engineer, Compute; Software Engineer, AI Gateway; Senior Software Engineer, Trust & Safety
