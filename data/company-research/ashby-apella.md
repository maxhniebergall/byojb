# Apella — research
Provider/key: ashby:apella | company_type: product

What they do: Apella provides AI-powered operating room (OR) intelligence software for hospitals and health systems. The platform uses ambient AI, computer vision, and electronic health record (EHR) integration to optimize OR capacity, reduce delays, improve case duration forecasting, and automate surgical event capture. Customers include Houston Methodist, Tampa General Hospital, and the Medical University of South Carolina. Claims up to 15x ROI through operational improvements.

How they describe themselves: "Applying computer vision and machine learning to improve the standard of care in the most critical aspect of healthcare: surgery." The company describes a "remote first culture with unique events to help integrate the team" and assists with home office setup. Founded 2020 in San Francisco, CA. Backed by prominent health-tech investors.

Size / stage / funding: Series B company. Raised $21M Series A (2021) + $80M Series B (January 2026) = ~$115M total raised. Series B led by HighlandX with participation from Vensana Capital, Casdin Capital, Houston Methodist (investor-customer). ~34 employees as of 2022 (grew 143% YoY at that point); current headcount likely 50-100+ given $80M raise.

Locations / HQ: San Francisco, CA. Remote-first culture.

Remote policy: Remote-first. Company assists with home office setup. All observed role postings list "United States, Remote" — no Canada mention found.

Remote-Canada eligibility: Unclear — likely US-only. Data Platform role is listed on Remote Rocketship as "United States, Remote." No Canada eligibility confirmed anywhere. Remote-first but US-scoped based on all available evidence.

Engineering & tech: Data platform role involves: dbt + Dagster for batch pipelines, BigQuery for data models, Kafka/PubSub + Flink (FlinkSQL) for real-time streaming pipelines. Backend role involves scalable service design and distributed systems. Healthcare-specific data workflows (EHR integration, OR telemetry). Stack: Python, SQL, BigQuery, Kafka, Flink, dbt, Dagster. Staff-level roles expected.

Notable / other: Recent $80M Series B (Jan 2026) positions them for significant expansion. Healthcare AI applied to OR operations is a regulated, high-stakes domain — more stable than consumer AI. Both investor and customer relationship with Houston Methodist is notable. Early-to-mid stage growth company post-large-funding-round.
