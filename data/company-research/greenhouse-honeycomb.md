# Honeycomb.io — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:honeycomb | company_type: product

What they do: Honeycomb is an observability and debugging platform for production software systems. It enables engineering teams to query, visualize, and understand the behavior of distributed systems in real time using high-cardinality, high-dimensionality event data. Their product is built around wide structured events (traces, logs, metrics) and a powerful query engine with no pre-aggregation and no cardinality limits. Used by tens of thousands of engineers.

How they describe themselves: "The AI observability platform for teams who build and manage software that matters." "Remote is here to stay. You're not going to see a 'back to office' email from us." "Really remote-first culture" with work-from-anywhere mindset, async communication norms, team offsites, and deep work days. Compensation is benchmarked to San Francisco market regardless of location. Regular semi-annual comp reviews tied to industry benchmarks and inflation.

Size / stage / funding: ~298 employees (as of April 2026). $150M raised over 6 rounds (Seed through Series D). Series D: $50M in April 2023. Late-stage private. Merged with/acquired Grit in April 2025. Founded 2016, San Francisco. HQ in San Francisco.

Locations / HQ: HQ in San Francisco, CA. Fully remote company — no office attendance required. Team distributed across US, Canada, Europe (Ireland, UK).

Remote policy: Fully remote-first and permanent. Company explicitly states "Remote is here to stay." Work-from-anywhere policy. Home setup stipend provided. Async communication emphasized. Team offsites held periodically.

Remote-Canada eligibility: Yes — verified. Job listings explicitly state "Canada Remote" for multiple engineering roles. Current open postings include "Senior Software Engineer II - App Observability Team (Remote - Canada)" on Greenhouse/Honeycomb careers. Senior SRE roles in Ireland and UK, suggesting genuine geographic distribution. Multiple Canada-tagged engineering positions confirmed on job boards.

Engineering & tech: Core platform is Go (backend ingestion, query engine, storage layer). Columnar storage system for high-cardinality event data. Query engine handles billions of events with P99 latency SLOs. Integrates with OpenTelemetry. New products (2025-2026): Honeycomb Private Cloud, Honeycomb Metrics, Canvas (AI-driven investigation), MCP Server integration, Anomaly Detection. Teams: App Observability, AI Intelligence, LLM Observability, SRE/platform.

Notable / other: Deep roots in distributed tracing and OpenTelemetry community. Charity Majors (CTO/co-founder) is widely recognized in the observability space.
