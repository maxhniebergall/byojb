# Chainguard — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:chainguard | company_type: product
What they do: Chainguard provides software supply chain security services, offering a suite of minimal, hardened, and secure container images (Chainguard Images) that are continuously updated to be free of known vulnerabilities.
How they describe themselves: Driven by the mission to be the "trusted source for open source." Their culture is built on four core pillars: being "Customer Obsessed," enjoying "Serious Work, Not Taking Ourselves Too Seriously," maintaining a "Bias for Intentional Action," and fostering high "Trust" (avoiding micromanagement). Their tone is modern, developer-centric, and security-focused.
Size / stage / funding: Private venture-backed startup, established growth stage (unicorn valuation), ~150-250 employees.
Locations / HQ: Bellevue, Washington (HQ), but fully distributed.
Remote policy: Fully remote-first since day one. They provide coworking stipends and support remote infrastructure.
Remote-Canada eligibility: Verified yes. They actively hire and support remote software engineering roles across Canada.
Engineering & tech: High focus on container technology, OCI standards, Linux distributions, security scanning, and build automation (The Chainguard Factory). Stack includes Go, Rust, Linux, Docker/Kubernetes, and open-source tooling (e.g., Sigstore, Cosign).
Notable / other: Hosts the "Unchained" tech blog covering supply-chain automation, vulnerability remediation, and container build pipelines. Founded by prominent open-source maintainers.
Open relevant roles (sample): Senior Systems Engineer, Image Factory; Infrastructure Security Engineer; Software Engineer, Platform.
