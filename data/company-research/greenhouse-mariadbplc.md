# MariaDB plc — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:mariadbplc | company_type: product

What they do: MariaDB is an open-source relational database company. Their flagship product is MariaDB Server, a community-developed fork of MySQL created by the original MySQL founder (Michael "Monty" Widenius) after Oracle acquired MySQL/Sun. MariaDB is the default database in most Linux distributions and has been downloaded over 3 billion times. They also offer SkySQL (cloud-hosted MariaDB), MariaDB Xpand (distributed SQL), and enterprise support. Serves mobile, web, IoT, and enterprise applications. Competes with Oracle MySQL and commercial RDBMS at lower cost.

How they describe themselves: "The cloud-native relational database that's been downloaded over 3 billion times." The company brings together "the brightest minds from Oracle, RedHat, HP, Sybase, Nokia, MySQL, Sun and Microsoft." Culture described as an "agile start-up company with a family ethos" despite its size. Values: global perspective through diverse backgrounds, smart/motivated people, boldness. The company emphasizes enabling customers to "unleash the power of their data."

Size / stage / funding: 200–400+ employees across 20+ countries. Previously public (NYSE: MRDB); taken private by K1 Investment Management in September 2024 (acquired at ~$0.55/share, ~$37M total — far below peak valuation of $445M at listing). K1 is a software-focused PE firm. Company had significant financial challenges leading to the acquisition (cash burn, stock crash from $445M to $12M valuation). New CEO appointed as part of acquisition.

Locations / HQ: Dual headquarters in Silicon Valley (CA) and Dublin, Ireland. Distributed team across 20+ countries. London office for sales/business roles. Engineering primarily remote.

Remote policy: Remote-first, globally distributed. ~87% of open roles are remote (20 of 23 jobs). Roles listed across India, US, Bulgaria, Romania, UK, South Korea, Vietnam, Malaysia, Canada.

Remote-Canada eligibility: PARTIAL — confirmed for support/enablement roles only. The only Canada-listed role is "KCS Knowledge Centered Service Enablement Expert (Remote - Canada)" — a support/enablement role, not engineering. The engineering roles (Principal Software Engineer - Distributed, Senior Software Engineer - Runtime) are listed as Remote - India only. No engineering roles currently show Canada eligibility.

Engineering & tech: C++ (core database engine — MySQL fork). Java (MariaDB MaxScale proxy). Python (tooling). The distributed database work (MariaDB Xpand) uses distributed SQL techniques. Runtime engineering (query execution, storage engine). Very deep systems programming, not web/cloud-native stack. Old-school database internals (C++/systems-level) rather than modern distributed infra.

Notable / other: Founded by Michael "Monty" Widenius, creator of MySQL. Open source community is the core of the company. K1 acquisition in 2024 followed near-bankruptcy — K1 acquired at a 189% premium to the then-depressed price but still at a fraction of the original valuation. PE ownership + prior financial distress = unclear long-term trajectory. The company has strong technical DNA (original MySQL team) but faces existential questions about competing with cloud-managed databases (AWS Aurora, Google Cloud SQL, PlanetScale).

Open relevant roles (sample): Principal Software Engineer - Distributed (Remote - India), Senior Software Engineer - Runtime (Remote - India), Software Engineer Customer Support
