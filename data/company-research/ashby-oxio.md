# OXIO Corporation — research
Provider/key: ashby:oxio | company_type: product

What they do: OXIO is the world's first Telecom-as-a-Service (TaaS) platform. They provide a 100% cloud-based, API-first platform that enables brands, enterprises, and operators to build and launch custom mobile connectivity products (MVNOs) without building their own telecom infrastructure. Their platform unbundles mobile telecom infrastructure and blends wireless infrastructure from multiple providers via APIs. Customers include banks, retailers, and other enterprises wanting to offer branded mobile services.

How they describe themselves: "Reimagine telecom for a limitless, connected tomorrow." "Technology-first approach to telecom." "API-first mindset" to overcome legacy industry constraints. Culture emphasizes employee voice: "We want your voice to be heard and encourage our teams to ask questions and share their ideas." Investors include Ascend, Atlantico, Collab Fund, Monashees, ParaFi Capital, Digital Currency Group, Multicoin Capital, FinTech Collective. Forbes Top Startup Employers #48 (2023). 2024 Telecoms Trailblazers award winner.

Size / stage / funding: Series B (~2022). Total raised ~$78.6M ($12M Series A 2020, $40M Series B 2022 led by ParaFi Capital). ~65–100 employees (small company). Most recent deal: merger/acquisition with Movistar Mexico (April 2026) — major strategic move into LATAM market. Expanding in US and Brazil.

Locations / HQ: Montreal, Quebec, Canada (confirmed office). New York City. Mexico City. Remote-first globally — "all employees work remotely" noted in multiple job listings.

Remote policy: Fully remote — all employees work remotely. This appears to be the primary work model. Physical offices in Montreal, NYC, Mexico City for optional use.
