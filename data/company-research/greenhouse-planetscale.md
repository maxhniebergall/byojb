# PlanetScale — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:planetscale | company_type: product
What they do: PlanetScale provides a serverless MySQL database platform built on Vitess, an open-source database clustering system. They specialize in high-performance, autoscaling database-as-a-service configurations with features like branch deployments and non-blocking schema migrations.
How they describe themselves: Grounded in a highly developer-centric culture that believes "every day matters" (focusing on momentum and urgency) and being "fearless." They emphasize team values of "no passengers" (high personal contribution) and "experts leading experts." They maintain a flat, highly autonomous environment with high ownership.
Size / stage / funding: Private venture-backed startup, established growth stage, ~100-200 employees.
Locations / HQ: Mountain View, California (HQ) with a fully distributed team.
Remote policy: Remote-first, globally distributed since inception.
Remote-Canada eligibility: Verified yes. They hire remote developers in Canada directly or via Employer of Record (PEO) services.
Engineering & tech: High focus on distributed systems database engines, query parsing, consensus protocols, and cluster scheduling. Tech stack is heavily Go (Golang) for Vitess and platform services, alongside C++, MySQL, and Kubernetes.
Notable / other: Maintains an active engineering blog (planetscale.com/blog) covering deep systems database internals, performance tuning, and schema migration strategies.
Open relevant roles (sample): Software Engineer, Vitess / Database Engines; SRE, Cloud Infrastructure; Platform Engineer.
