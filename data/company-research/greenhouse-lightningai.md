# Lightning AI — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:lightningai | company_type: product

What they do: Lightning AI is the company behind PyTorch Lightning (open-source ML training framework) and an AI cloud platform. They provide GPU cloud infrastructure — collaborative workspaces, persistent GPU notebooks, managed training and inference clusters, and pay-per-token APIs for AI models. In 2025–2026 they merged with Voltage Park to create what they describe as "the first cloud built for AI," combining software tooling with direct GPU data center operations.

How they describe themselves: "Turn ideas into AI, Lightning fast." Mission: reduce friction in the AI development lifecycle from research to production. Engineering culture described as hybrid (2 days/week in office from NYC, London, or Palo Alto), with 5 weeks/year of fully remote-from-anywhere flexibility. Team is approximately 50 people, with 25 in product/technology.

Size / stage / funding: ~50 employees. Founded 2019. Total funding: $58.6M from Coatue, Index Ventures, Bain Capital Ventures, and Firstminute. Private/growth stage. No office in Canada.

Locations / HQ: New York City (primary), San Francisco/Palo Alto, Seattle, London. Data center operations in Fort Worth TX, Lisle IL, Quincy WA (post-Voltage Park merger).

Remote policy: Hybrid — employees work from NYC, London, or Palo Alto offices ~2 days/week. 5 weeks/year can work from anywhere. Not a remote-first company despite some remote job listings. Infrastructure engineer roles list "Remote" alongside NY/SF/Seattle as options, but the company's culture expects regular office presence for most employees.

Remote-Canada eligibility: No — not verified. No Canadian offices. No Canadian locations mentioned in any job posting. Infrastructure roles listed as "Remote" appear to mean US-remote (NY/SF/Seattle corridor). The company uses an EOR for international employees in some contexts but no evidence of active Canadian hiring for engineering roles.

Engineering & tech: GPU infrastructure at the intersection of ML tooling and cloud operations. Teams: Network Operations Center (data center NOC roles), Infrastructure Engineering (GPU & Compute, Observability, Storage), Backend Engineering, Research Engineering. Stack includes PyTorch ecosystem, Kubernetes, distributed training infrastructure, cloud networking. Post-Voltage Park merger, the company now operates physical GPU data centers alongside the software platform.

Notable / other: PyTorch Lightning has 28,000+ GitHub stars and is widely used in ML research. The Voltage Park merger significantly expanded their infrastructure footprint. Small team (~50) means high individual impact. Coatue and Index Ventures backing provides strong financial runway. Engineering roles in infrastructure are genuinely infrastructure/platform work, not AI hype. However, the core product is an AI development platform — adjacent to AI tooling rather than pure infrastructure.

Open relevant roles (sample): Backend Engineer; Infrastructure Engineer (GPU & Compute); Infrastructure Engineer (Observability); Infrastructure Engineer (Storage).
