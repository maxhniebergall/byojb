# Persona — research
Provider/key: ashby:persona | company_type: product

What they do: Persona (withpersona.com) is an identity verification and identity infrastructure platform. They provide automated government ID verification, KYC/AML/KYB compliance tooling, fraud detection, and continuous identity assurance for businesses. Customers include OpenAI, DoorDash, Coursera, and Lime. They enable businesses to "collect, verify, and manage user identities throughout the customer lifecycle" through a composable, API-first platform.

How they describe themselves: "Making the internet safer by automating any identity-related use case with flexible identity infrastructure." Team mission: help fellow teammates succeed + make the internet a safer place. Culture described as "incredibly kind and supportive." Engineering alumni from Square, Uber, Coursera, LinkedIn, Dropbox, and Slack. Offers a monthly coworking stipend for fully remote employees, suggesting they value remote culture but want in-person connection available.

Size / stage / funding: ~685 employees (Apr 2026). Founded 2018. Series D (Apr 2025, $200M round). Total funding: ~$418M from 14 investors including Founders Fund, Coatue, Index Ventures, First Round Capital. Valuation: $2B (May 2025). HQ: San Francisco, CA.

Locations / HQ: San Francisco, CA (HQ). Secondary office presence in Toronto, ON (Canada). Remote US positions available. Some roles specifically list "Remote - Toronto, Canada."

Remote policy: Mix of remote and office. Remote employees receive a monthly coworking stipend. Some roles specifically list US remote; others list Toronto, Canada remote. Infrastructure roles explicitly specify "Remote — US only" (teammates in each US time zone, quarterly visit to SF HQ). Product roles have had Canada-remote variants (Toronto).

Remote-Canada eligibility: Partial / role-dependent. Evidence found of a "Software Engineer, Product (Remote - Toronto, Canada)" posting with Canadian benefits (RRSP, medical/dental/vision). However, infrastructure and compute roles (the most relevant to this candidate) are documented as "Remote — US only," with US team distribution. A Software Engineer, Compute role listing on BuiltIn explicitly stated "Hiring Remotely in USA" only. In practice: product/generalist roles may be open to Toronto/Canada; infrastructure/security/resilience/compute roles appear US-only as of recent postings.

Engineering & tech: Identity infrastructure at scale — real-time verification pipelines, fraud detection systems, secure document processing. Teams include: Infrastructure (scalability, resilience, cost), Compute, Security, Resilience, Edge, Data Infrastructure. Core platform is API-first, composable, with ML-assisted fraud detection. Engineering org draws from fintech and enterprise SaaS backgrounds. Stack not publicly detailed but implied: cloud-native (AWS/GCP), distributed systems, security-focused.

Notable / other: One of the more technically interesting identity infrastructure companies (not a commodity KYC vendor). Significant growth — from 300 employees to 685 in ~2 years. Series D at $2B valuation positions them as a late-stage private company. Age verification controversy in 2026 (reports of filing government reports on users) — context to monitor. Has a "continuous workforce identity assurance" product targeting enterprise HR.
