# Check Technologies — research
Provider/key: ashby:check-technologies | company_type: product

What they do: Check Technologies (branded as "Check") is an embedded payroll infrastructure company. They provide APIs and platform components that allow software businesses (vertical SaaS, HR tech, workforce management, accounting platforms) to build and operate native payroll products for their own customers. Check handles payroll processing, tax calculations, remittance and filing, real-time payments, benefits administration (health, 401k), and workers' compensation. Processes $15B+ annually for 35,000+ businesses via 65+ platform partners.

How they describe themselves: "Make paying people simple." "We're building payroll businesses, together." Check positions itself as infrastructure-layer payroll—analogous to Stripe for payments but for payroll. They recently launched Check MCP (Model Context Protocol) and a developer CLI tool, signaling a developer-first posture. Engineers collaborate closely with legal, compliance, and tax operations teams given the complexity of the payroll domain.

Size / stage / funding: ~90 employees (2026). Series B/C stage startup (exact round not publicly confirmed). Notable: the company laid off approximately 20% of staff at some point, citing revenue growth that has not yet materialized as expected. Offices in New York City (HQ) and San Francisco. Revenue trajectory uncertain; one May 2026 Glassdoor review called it "the best job I've ever had" while others noted the layoffs.

Locations / HQ: New York City (HQ) and San Francisco offices. Team is described as "distributed across the US." Flexible/remote options available. No Canadian office.

Remote policy: In-office, hybrid, and remote options all available. "Flexible" on-site time. The team is described as US-distributed; no explicit statement about hiring outside the US.

Remote-Canada eligibility: Unclear / probably US-only. The distributed team is described as "across the US," salaries quoted in USD from US employees, offices are US-only. No evidence of Canadian employees or a Canadian entity. Likely US-only remote.

Engineering & tech: Payroll infrastructure/API company. Stack includes Ruby (inferred from partner job listings), Python, distributed systems for financial processing, compliance integrations. Staff Software Engineer, Infrastructure role targets platform reliability and scalability of the payroll infrastructure. Fintech, HR tech, infrastructure-as-a-service positioning. Engineers work with compliance/legal/tax ops—cross-functional but specialized domain.
