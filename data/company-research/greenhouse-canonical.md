# Canonical — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:canonical | company_type: product
What they do: Canonical develops and supports Ubuntu, the leading open-source operating system, alongside tools for cloud infrastructure, Kubernetes, IoT, and database management. They provide commercial support, security maintenance, and services (such as Ubuntu Pro) to enterprises.
How they describe themselves: Guided by the mission to "level the playing field" by bringing free, high-quality open-source software to everyone. Their culture is built around Ubuntu's core philosophy ("humanity to others"), emphasizing interconnectedness, excellence, and a commitment to precision. Their tone is structured, professional, and dedicated to technical excellence.
Size / stage / funding: Established, private, profitable, ~1,000+ employees.
Locations / HQ: Headquartered in London, UK, but fully distributed globally.
Remote policy: Fully remote and distributed since 2004, supplemented by twice-yearly global sprints.
Remote-Canada eligibility: Verified yes. They actively hire and employ remote workers across Canada.
Engineering & tech: Heavy focus on low-level systems, kernel development, packaging, OS tooling, containers, and distributed databases. Stack includes Python, Go, C/C++, Rust, bash, and Ubuntu/Debian packaging systems.
Notable / other: The recruitment process is famous for being exceptionally long and rigorous, featuring aptitude tests, written personality questionnaires/essays, and multiple interviews.
Open relevant roles (sample): Systems Engineer, Database Operations; Software Engineer, Canonical Kubernetes; Linux Systems Engineer.
Sources: canonical.com, canonical.com/careers, ubuntu.com.
