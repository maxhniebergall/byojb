# Databricks — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:databricks | company_type: product

What they do: Databricks is a data and AI company building the Data Intelligence Platform — a unified lakehouse platform combining data engineering, data warehousing, ML training, and AI development. Core products: Apache Spark (original open-source foundation), Delta Lake, MLflow, and the Databricks platform. Used by thousands of enterprises for large-scale data processing, analytics, and AI model training. Pre-IPO stage; one of the most valuable private tech companies.

How they describe themselves: "Innovators, builders and truthseekers" helping "the world's largest companies use better data to build smarter AI." Values: innovation, autonomy ("a true sense of autonomy, driving initiatives from zero through completion"), rapid impact ("ship features used by thousands of enterprises within weeks"), open source commitment, and collaboration with industry leaders. Engineering culture emphasizes shipping at scale and working with Databricks founders directly.

Size / stage / funding: ~7,000+ employees. Founded 2013 by the original Apache Spark team from UC Berkeley AMPLab. Pre-IPO / late growth stage. Total funding: ~$4B+. Valuation: ~$62B (2024 funding round). Revenue: $1.6B ARR (2024), growing rapidly. HQ: San Francisco, CA. R&D centers in SF, Mountain View, Seattle, Bellevue, Amsterdam, Serbia, Berlin.

Locations / HQ: San Francisco CA (HQ), Mountain View CA, Seattle WA, Bellevue WA, Amsterdam, Serbia, Berlin. No listed Canadian offices.

Remote policy: Hybrid-first with strict enforcement for most engineering roles. Policy as of 2026: most engineers required 3 days/week in office, with badge tracking enforced (weekly badge reports sent to employees and managers). Fully remote roles rare — reserved for Staff-level+ or sales/professional services. New 305,000 sq ft campus opening in Sunnyvale in late 2026; new SF HQ at One Sansome Street. Company is betting heavily on in-person culture long-term.

Remote-Canada eligibility: No — not supported for direct hire. No Canadian office. Policy states that employees in countries where Databricks has no office would need to be contractors or work through a third-party employer of record (EOR) arrangement — not a standard direct employment option. Confirmed via jobsbyculture.com research citing official policy. The job listings found for "Databricks Canada" on LinkedIn/Indeed are for positions at companies that use Databricks (the tool), not jobs at Databricks Inc. itself.

Engineering & tech: Deep data infrastructure engineering — distributed systems (Spark, Delta Lake), compute infrastructure, AI runtime (model serving, inference), application traffic management, backend services, and distributed data systems. ML/AI platform including GenAI capabilities. Tech stack: Scala, Python, Java, Go, Kubernetes, cloud-native (AWS, Azure, GCP). Roles include: Senior Software Engineers in Compute Infrastructure, Distributed Data Systems, AI Runtime, Application Traffic, Backend. Strong open-source culture (Delta Lake, MLflow are company-originated OSS projects).

Notable / other: One of the most technically impressive data/AI infrastructure companies in the world. Pre-IPO with $62B valuation — IPO expected but no confirmed date as of mid-2026. Intense engineering culture with high performance expectations. The in-office enforcement is a significant recent shift. Apache Spark's success drives massive credibility and enterprise adoption. Acquisition of Tabular (Delta/Iceberg company) in 2024 strengthened the lakehouse position.

Open relevant roles (sample): Senior Machine Learning Engineer - GenAI Platform; Senior Software Engineer - AI Platform (NYC); Senior Software Engineer, AI Runtime; Senior Software Engineer - Application Traffic team; Senior Software Engineer - Backend; Senior Software Engineer (Backend) - AI/ML Environments; Senior Software Engineer, Compute Infrastructure; Senior Software Engineer - Distributed Data Systems.
