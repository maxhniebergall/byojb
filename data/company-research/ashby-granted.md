# Granted — research
Provider/key: ashby:granted | company_type: product

What they do: Granted (formerly Medbill AI) is a healthcare benefits navigation and medical billing platform. Their mobile app (iOS) helps employees and patients navigate medical claims, understand their health benefits, check and negotiate medical bills, and manage healthcare costs. The company partners with employers through benefits channels (e.g., March 2026 partnership with StretchDollar) to offer bill management to employees as a benefit.

How they describe themselves: Mission centers on simplifying healthcare navigation — "helping people navigate health benefits and medical bills." The company focuses on saving patients time and money on healthcare costs through AI-powered tools. Based in New York's Chelsea neighborhood.

Size / stage / funding: Seed stage. ~23 employees as of 2026. Raised ~$17M total (single round, June 2024, described as "mid-teens of millions" seed). Active hiring across 8 positions including engineering, product, and AI/ML roles.

Locations / HQ: New York City (Chelsea). Hiring described as both remote and in-person at NYC HQ. No Canadian presence identified.

Remote policy: Hiring both remotely and at NYC HQ, per their Built In profile. Specific location requirements for engineering roles not confirmed — the Ashby job board returns minimal content when fetched (JS-heavy), making role-level verification difficult.

Remote-Canada eligibility: Unclear. The company is US-based seed-stage startup with 23 employees and NYC HQ. No evidence of Canadian employees, Canadian entity, or explicit remote-Canada policy. Probable US-focused remote, but not confirmed either way.

Engineering & tech: AI/ML-heavy product (healthcare AI, claims parsing, benefits navigation). Engineering roles listed include Backend Engineer (Agentic AI, Staff+ Level) and Infrastructure Engineer (Senior/Staff Level). Likely Python-heavy backend; agentic AI pipelines; possibly Node.js. Very small engineering team (~8-12 engineers estimated). Infrastructure role would be foundational given early stage.

Notable / other: Rebranded from Medbill AI to Granted in late 2024. Recent partnership with StretchDollar (March 2026) suggests active business development. Healthcare AI/benefits is a crowded space.
