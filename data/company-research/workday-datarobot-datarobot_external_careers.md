# DataRobot — research
Provider/key: workday:datarobot/datarobot_external_careers | company_type: product

What they do: DataRobot provides an enterprise AI platform (now rebranded as "Unified Agent Workforce Platform") that enables organizations to build, operate, and govern AI agents at scale. The platform spans generative AI, predictive AI, AI governance, and observability. Originally focused on AutoML and predictive modeling; pivoted toward agentic AI in 2024–2025. Integrations with enterprise systems including SAP. Targets Fortune 500 enterprises.

How they describe themselves: "Make AI matter" — helping shape how AI works in real-world enterprise settings. "Define your path. Defy the limits" — emphasizing internal career development. "Big enough to matter. Small enough to move" — positioning as an established but agile leader. "Headquartered in Boston, built to work anywhere." Offers "Hybrid & remote work" as a stated benefit. Has been recognized by Gartner Magic Quadrant for DSML Platforms (multiyear leader), Forbes Cloud 100, AI 50, Fortune Future 50.

Size / stage / funding: Series G (2021, $300M at $6.3B valuation). ~869 employees as of mid-2026. Total funding ~$1.1B. Significant layoffs in 2022 (26% workforce cut, then additional 7% earlier that year) amid missed revenue targets. Since then restructured; $285M ARR as of 2024. History of executive turmoil (stock sale controversy) documented in Pragmatic Engineer newsletter.

Locations / HQ: Boston MA. Website lists US and Japan (jp) as selector options. No Canadian office. Remote hiring noted — at least one Senior Software Engineer role listed as "Canada remote" found in search results.

Remote policy: "Built to work anywhere" — describes hybrid and remote work as a benefit. Actively hiring for remote positions globally. Senior Software Engineer Canada remote posting confirmed in search results (exact date unclear).

Remote-Canada eligibility: Likely yes for engineering roles — "Canada remote" Senior Software Engineer posting found. Platform/backend engineering roles appear to include Canadian candidates. Confirm per listing.

Engineering & tech: AI platform engineering, agent orchestration frameworks, backend services (Python heavy), data connectors, fleet management infrastructure, database engineering, DevOps/infrastructure. Stack: Python, cloud-native, likely AWS/Azure/GCP. Product is an enterprise SaaS ML/AI platform, not infrastructure for infrastructure's sake — closer to application-layer ML tooling.

Notable / other: Long history of turbulence: multiple rounds of layoffs (2022), insider stock sale controversy that caused internal rebellion (documented by Pragmatic Engineer). Company pivoted from AutoML/predictive to agentic AI following market trends. Despite turbulence, $285M ARR and Gartner Magic Quadrant leadership suggest continued market relevance. Founder Dharmesh Shah left; multiple CEO changes. Company has stabilized somewhat but carries reputational baggage.

Open relevant roles (sample): Senior Backend Engineer, Senior Python Software Engineer (Data & Connectors), Principal Software Engineer, Staff Software Engineer, DevOps Engineer II
