# Risepoint — research
Provider/key: workday:risepoint/risepoint | company_type: product

What they do: Risepoint (formerly Academic Partnerships, rebranded May 2024) is an Online Program Management (OPM) company that partners with 100+ not-for-profit regional universities across the US, UK, and APAC to develop, launch, and scale affordable online degree programs. They provide marketing, enrollment, and student-success services to universities in exchange for a revenue-share model. Core focus areas include nursing, education, business, public service, and technology programs.

How they describe themselves: "Risepoint is an education services company that provides expertise to guide growth for universities and their students." Mission: "increase access to life-changing education." They frame themselves as a mission-driven partner to universities—40% of their own employees are first-generation college students, 15% have enrolled in Risepoint-supported programs. Tone is mission-forward and values-oriented around educational access.

Size / stage / funding: Private, PE-backed. Owned by Vistria Group (acquired 2019 via Vistria II fund). Raised ~$90M total from investors including Atlantic Merchant Capital, Ascension Ventures, Claritas Capital. ~1,500+ global employees. In Jan 2024 acquired Wiley University Services (higher-ed division of John Wiley & Sons) for $150M, significantly expanding their university network and international footprint. Subject of growing regulatory scrutiny and contract terminations in the OPM sector (147 partnerships ended in 2023; new 2024 contracts down 50%+).

Locations / HQ: Dallas, Texas (HQ). Remote-first across US, Canada, UK, Australia.

Remote policy: Explicitly remote-first. Employees located across the US, Canada, UK, and Australia.

Remote-Canada eligibility: Yes — Risepoint explicitly states a remote-first model operating across Canada. Canadian employees confirmed.

Engineering & tech: Not publicly detailed. Technical roles span data analytics, business intelligence, machine learning, data science, predictive analytics, and platform engineering. Workday ATS suggests standard enterprise HR/tech stack.

Notable / other: The company is facing significant headwinds in its industry — the OPM sector is contracting as DOE scrutiny increases, tuition-share agreements face regulatory challenges, and criticism from consumer advocates and educators mounts. The company was criticized for aggressive recruitment practices targeting low-income students. The "rise and fall" framing from higher-education press suggests strategic instability. Rebranded from "Academic Partnerships" to "Risepoint" in 2024 amid these pressures.

Open relevant roles (sample): Senior Software Engineer; Principal Software Architect Generative AI & Platform Engineering
