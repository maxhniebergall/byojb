# Playlab — research
Provider/key: ashby:playlab | company_type: product

What they do: Playlab is a nonprofit AI education platform that enables educators, nonprofits, and impact organizations to build their own AI-powered tools and learning experiences without requiring deep technical knowledge. The platform is "open" on three layers: open applications (educators learn from each other), open software (transparent and extendable), and open AI infrastructure (users can choose which AI models power their work). Serves 7.3M+ students across four continents. In 2026, partnered with Amazon ($800k investment) to expand reach to 500,000 students.

How they describe themselves: "We put the power of AI into the hands of people who are already building the most human part of our future — teachers, students and impact organizations." Nonprofit committed to "responsible, open-source AI work." The team is "small, intensely collaborative" and values educators as primary stakeholders. Mission-driven with a focus on equity and access in AI education.

Size / stage / funding: Nonprofit, founded 2023, San Francisco. Small team (~10 engineers, doubling to ~20 in 2026 per Head of Engineering posting). Not VC-funded in traditional sense; funded via grants, philanthropic support, Amazon partnership, and nonprofit channels. Amazon invested $800k via educational partnership.

Locations / HQ: San Francisco, CA (HQ). Jobs are listed as "United States" remote on Fast Forward nonprofit job board and RemoteRocketship lists as "US remote."

Remote policy: Remote within the United States. Job postings list "United States" as the location, indicating US-only remote. The Staff ML Engineer posting explicitly lists "United States" as the geographic scope.

Remote-Canada eligibility: No (likely). All evidence points to US-only remote — nonprofit with US-only staff, job postings listing "United States," no Canadian presence identified. As a nonprofit relying on grant funding and US-based operations, hiring outside the US would require additional legal/compliance overhead unlikely for a ~10-person org.

Engineering & tech: Python (ML/data pipelines), Node.js/TypeScript (web platform), generative AI (RAG, LLMs, multi-model workflows), MLOps and observability. Infrastructure role targets systems for scaling educational AI applications. ML Engineer role focuses on RAG improvements, model integration, privacy/safety features, and educational AI pipelines. Small, collaborative team building AI-native educational tools.

Notable / other: Fast Company's Most Innovative Companies (2026 recognition). Amazon Education partnership. Strong mission story — genuine nonprofit impact. Team is small and growing fast; the Head of Engineering posting aimed to double the team from ~10 to 20 in 2026.
