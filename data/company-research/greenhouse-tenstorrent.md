# Tenstorrent — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:tenstorrent | company_type: product

What they do: Tenstorrent designs RISC-V CPUs and Tensor AI processors (accelerators) for AI training and inference workloads. They build both hardware (chips, workstations, compute servers) and the software stack (compilers, ML frameworks, RISC-V software ecosystem) needed to run AI models on their silicon. Their product line includes the Blackhole AI chip, TT-QuietBox workstations, and Tenstorrent Galaxy servers.

How they describe themselves: "An open future for AI — open means affordable, transparent, and modifiable." Company mission is to challenge NVIDIA's dominance in AI compute through open RISC-V architecture. "High end CPUs and AI work better together, so we build both." Led by legendary chip designer Jim Keller.

Size / stage / funding: Well-funded startup, considered late-stage / pre-IPO. Raised hundreds of millions; Samsung and SoftBank are known investors. In 2026, Tenstorrent attracted takeover interest from Intel and Qualcomm (Bloomberg, May 2026), suggesting significant valuation. Estimated 500-1000+ employees.

Locations / HQ: HQ in Toronto, Ontario, Canada (also listed as Santa Clara, CA). Additional offices: Austin TX, Fort Collins CO, Boston MA, Bengaluru India, Beijing China, Pangyo South Korea, Tokyo Japan, Belgrade Serbia, Munich Germany, Warsaw Poland.

Remote policy: Mixed. Most roles on their job board list specific office locations without remote tags. However, some remote roles do exist (e.g., "Remote Staff Engineer Software Release and Packaging RISC-V" on RemoteOK; Sr. Engineer Systems Canada remote on Built In Toronto). Hybrid roles exist in Toronto.

Remote-Canada eligibility: Partial / unclear. Tenstorrent has a major Toronto office and 18 Canada job listings on Glassdoor as of May 2026. Some roles are explicitly hybrid in Toronto. A subset of roles appear to be remote within Canada. The roles in the batch (C++ ML Engineer, RISC-V, AI Compiler, Kernel Dev) are predominantly office-based or Toronto-hybrid based on job board data; fully remote-Canada eligibility is not confirmed for most software engineering roles.

Engineering & tech: Hardware: RISC-V CPUs, Tensor AI accelerators, chip architecture. Software: AI compiler (MLIR-based), ML model bring-up, kernel development, RISC-V software stack, HPC and agentic software. Languages: C++, Python. Tech partners: TSMC, Samsung, Rapidus for 2nm manufacturing.

Notable / other: Jim Keller (ex-AMD, Apple, Intel) is CEO. Company attracted Intel and Qualcomm acquisition interest in May 2026. New hardware (QuietBox 2 at $9,999) released Q2 2026. Galaxy Blackhole GA announced April 2026. Active open-source RISC-V contributor.

Open relevant roles (sample): C++ Machine Learning Engineer (AI Models Training), RISC-V AI/HPC & Agentic Software Engineer, Software Engineer AI Compiler, Software Engineer AI Model Bring Up, Software Engineer Kernel Development and Optimization
