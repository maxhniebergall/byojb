# Vultr — research
Provider/key: ashby:vultr | company_type: product
What they do: Vultr (operated by Constant) is a cloud computing provider offering SSD cloud compute, bare metal servers, block storage, and cloud GPU clusters. They compete directly with major public cloud hosts (AWS, GCP, DigitalOcean) by focusing on simple, low-cost, high-performance edge infrastructure.
How they describe themselves: Guided by the mission to make high-performance cloud compute and GPU infrastructure easy to use, affordable, and locally accessible globally. They emphasize core values of passion, collaboration, and education, supporting continuous learning with professional development reimbursements.
Size / stage / funding: Established, private company, ~200+ employees.
Locations / HQ: West Palm Beach, Florida (HQ) with global data centers.
Remote policy: Remote-first, but with geographical hiring limits.
Remote-Canada eligibility: No. While they operate under a remote-first culture, they do not hire or support remote software engineering roles in Canada. Job postings for remote positions are restricted to "Remote - United States."
Engineering & tech: High focus on bare metal hosting, hypervisors, virtualization, networking protocols, and GPU cluster scheduling. Technologies include C, C++, Go, Python, and custom Linux virtualization pipelines.
Notable / other: Maintains an active news/blog section focusing on deploying cloud services, AI/GPU acceleration workloads, and multi-cloud infrastructure designs.
Open relevant roles (sample): Senior Infrastructure Engineer; SRE, Cloud Platforms; GPU Systems Engineer.
