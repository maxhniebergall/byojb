# Versapay — research
Provider/key: lever:versapay | company_type: product

What they do: Versapay provides accounts receivable (AR) automation software that helps businesses automate the entire invoice-to-cash process — digital invoicing, customer portals, AI-powered cash application, collections management, reporting/reconciliation, and B2B payment processing. Serves manufacturing, wholesale distribution, and professional services companies. Over 10,000 customers, 5M+ companies on their B2B payment network, processing 110M+ transactions and $257B annually (updated: $170B in older data; current figure is $257B per recent press).

How they describe themselves: "Remove financial friction so that business ecosystems transact smarter, faster, and with greater resiliency." The company emphasizes turning AR into a competitive advantage. Five guiding values: own your impact, show grit, unite to win, act with courage and candor, and energize/build joy. Fully remote with no return-to-office mandate.

Size / stage / funding: ~359 employees (2026). Founded 2006 in Vancouver, BC by Michael Gokturk and Kevin Short. IPO'd on TSX (2016), then acquired by Great Hill Partners (private equity) for $95.6M in December 2019. Also acquired DadeSystems (US fintech) in April 2022. Currently private, PE-backed. Revenue ~$42M (2024).

Locations / HQ: Originally Vancouver, BC; current HQ Miami, FL (post-PE acquisition). Fully remote — no physical offices required. Team spans Canada, United States, and Israel.

Remote policy: Fully remote, no return-to-office mandate. The team is explicitly distributed across Canada, the United States, and Israel. Employees manage their own workflow and schedule with trust and autonomy. Virtual events, in-person town halls, and team meetups provided.

Remote-Canada eligibility: Yes (confirmed). The careers page and multiple job postings explicitly list "Canada (Remote)" as a work location. QA Engineer, Senior Software Engineer, and Engineering Lead roles all have Canada remote postings on Canadian job boards (eluta.ca, communitech.ca, Himalayas). The company was founded in Canada and retains Canadian operations.

Engineering & tech: .NET/C# (legacy payments platform, active Principal .NET Software Engineer role), Ruby on Rails (web platform), distributed payments infrastructure, AWS cloud, ERP integrations (NetSuite, Sage Intacct, Microsoft Dynamics 365), AI/ML for cash application. The Principal .NET Software Engineer - Cloud Payments Platform role targets modernizing payment infrastructure, improving scalability and security, and designing new distributed services.

Notable / other: Canadian-founded company (Vancouver, BC) with active Canadian remote hiring. PE-backed (Great Hill Partners) — profitable/revenue-generating business model, not venture growth-at-all-costs. Strong established customer base. The Cloud Payments Platform role is genuine backend/distributed systems infrastructure work. .NET stack may be a consideration depending on Max's preferences.
