# OpenSesame — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:opensesame | company_type: product

What they do: OpenSesame is a corporate eLearning marketplace and training platform. They operate an online marketplace with 60,000+ skill-building courses and learning pathways from 1,000+ content publishers, covering compliance, soft skills, industry-specific training, and professional development across 70+ languages. Founded 2011. Serves enterprise customers for workforce development.

How they describe themselves: "Unlock every employee's full potential." Mission-driven edtech company. Culture emphasizes diversity and inclusion across "race, gender, religion, sexual orientation, veteran status, age, and identity." Employee Resource Groups (ERGs). 40 annual hours for professional development per employee. 100% 401(k) matching (up to 4%). Flexible PTO. One volunteer day annually. "Embrace a remote-first approach that empowers our employees to live and work where they thrive."

Size / stage / funding: ~330 employees (May 2026). Revenue $33.9M (2025). Founded 2011 by Tom Turnbull and Asok Chattopadhyay; current CEO Don Spear. Total funding: $102M across 6 rounds (Series D as of July 2021 — $50M led by JMI Equity; also FTV Capital). Portland, OR HQ.

Locations / HQ: Portland, Oregon, USA. Fully remote workforce.

Remote policy: Remote-first. "Embrace a remote-first approach that empowers employees to live and work where they thrive." All-company in-person meetings several times per year.

Remote-Canada eligibility: Yes — confirmed. Explicit "Senior DevOps Software Engineer - Remote (Canada)" and "Software Engineer, LATAM & Canada" job listings on their Greenhouse board. A "Senior Software Engineer (Canada)" listing also on Greenhouse confirms active Canada-remote hiring.

Engineering & tech: Stack includes TypeScript, Node.js, GitHub Actions, Terraform, AWS, SQL, SAML/OIDC. The DevOps role involves cloud infrastructure, automation, and platform work. 44+ technologies in use including Cloudflare CDN. The platform is SaaS/marketplace architecture.

Notable / other: Mature, stable Series D edtech company — not a hype startup. Product is corporate training, not AI-native. Engineering team focused on platform reliability, DevOps, and integrations. The role titled "Data Engineering Lead" suggests data pipeline work. Not a pure infrastructure/systems company, but the DevOps roles are legitimate platform engineering.
