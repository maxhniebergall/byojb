# AIS — research

What they do: Applied Information Sciences (AIS) is an employee-owned IT consulting and professional services firm specializing in Microsoft cloud transformation, DevOps, data & AI, security, modern work (M365), and infrastructure modernization. They serve large commercial enterprises and federal government agencies (US Air Force, DHS, etc.). Founded 1982, headquartered in Reston, Virginia.

How they describe themselves: "Our success can only come from the success of our clients." Mission-focused, outcome-oriented. Employee-owned since inception — every team member has direct financial stakes in client outcomes. Describe themselves as a "Trusted Cloud Solutions Partner" and "Microsoft cloud solutions partner." Six capability pillars: Data & AI, Security & Compliance, Modern Work, Digital & App Innovation, Business Applications, Infrastructure Modernization.

Size / stage / funding: ~1,000-5,000 employees across 20+ US locations. Employee-owned (ESOP). Nearly 40 years in operation. Private company with stable, established client base.

Locations / HQ: Reston, Virginia (HQ). Additional US offices in Indiana, Texas, Ohio, North Carolina, and other locations. All US-based. International project work only in Mauritania, Morocco, Venezuela (specific roles).

Remote policy: Some positions offer remote work with occasional travel. However, all 16 current remote job listings are restricted to the United States. No Canada hiring. Federal government work may require US security clearances (Secret clearance mentioned).

Remote-Canada eligibility: No — AIS is a US-based consulting firm with no Canadian offices or Canada-eligible positions. All 16 current remote listings specify United States as the eligibility requirement. Federal government clientele further restricts to US persons.

Engineering & tech: Microsoft-centric tech stack: Azure, M365, Power Platform, Teams Rooms, Dynamics. Also: Kubernetes Platform Engineering, Oracle Cloud Infrastructure, DevSecOps. Cloud engineering and application integration. Work is client-facing consulting — engineers are assigned to client projects, not internal product development.

Notable / other: AIS is a consulting/professional services firm — not a product company. Work involves being placed on client engagements (government and enterprise). This is a fundamentally different work model than product engineering. Roles like "Kubernetes Platform Engineer" and "Azure Cloud Infrastructure Engineer" are consulting roles delivered to clients. Secret clearance required for some federal roles, implying US citizenship/residency.

Open relevant roles (sample): Kubernetes Platform Engineer (US only), Azure Cloud Infrastructure Engineer Sr. (US only), Sr. Infrastructure Engineer (US only), Data Engineer (US only), Senior Oracle Cloud Infrastructure Architect (US only)
