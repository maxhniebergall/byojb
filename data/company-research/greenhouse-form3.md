# Form3 — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:form3 | company_type: product

What they do: Form3 is a cloud-native payment infrastructure company providing Payments-as-a-Service for banks and financial institutions. Their platform enables access to payment clearing and settlement schemes across the US (FedNow, TCH RTP — real-time instant payments), EU, and UK through a single API integration. Customers include Lloyds Bank, Barclays, J.P. Morgan, Klarna, and N26. The platform is multi-cloud (AWS, GCP, Azure), ISO 27001 and SOC 2 Type II certified.

How they describe themselves: "Revolutionising the way payments work from channel to payment scheme." They position themselves as "payment technology reimagined" — cloud-native, API-first, always-on, highly reliable. Culture emphasis: remote-first, flexible, learning-focused, wellbeing-centered, inclusive. Annual L&D allowance, up to 6 paid learning days/year. Summer hours. Mental health support. Part-time and job sharing supported.

Size / stage / funding: ~222–350 employees (sources vary). Founded 2016 in London. Total funding: $278M over 9 rounds from 33 investors. Series C closed at $220M, valuation $570M (2024, led by British Patient Capital with Visa, Goldman Sachs, and Mastercard participating). Additional strategic investment from Nationwide + BlackRock debt facility in October 2025 to accelerate US growth. Record results in H1 2025.

Locations / HQ: Headquarters in London, UK. 85+ nationalities across 26 countries. Primary geographies: UK, Europe (Germany, Netherlands, Spain, Portugal), US, Canada (East timezone), Argentina. Remote-first globally.

Remote policy: 100% remote for all engineering roles. The company explicitly states "manage your schedule and work from wherever you are happiest and most productive!" Part-time and flexible arrangements widely available. Engineering operates across UK/EU and expanding into North America.

Remote-Canada eligibility: YES — confirmed, with caveat. Form3 is actively building an engineering team in Canada for their US Instant Payments product (FedNow/TCH RTP). Their Canada hiring is currently focused on **East Canada time zones** (Ontario, Quebec, Atlantic, Newfoundland) — described as hiring engineers "based in Canada East timezones or based in Canada and able to work close to GMT-4." Kimberley BC (Mountain Time, GMT-7) is a West Canada timezone and may fall outside the stated preference for East Canada. However, the company has not explicitly excluded West Canada — this should be clarified with the recruiter.

Engineering & tech: Go (primary language for all engineering roles). Microservices architecture. Multi-cloud (AWS, GCP, Azure). Event-driven, distributed systems. Real-time payment processing with strict reliability and compliance requirements. US Instant Payments team focuses on FedNow and TCH RTP integration. Strong emphasis on resilience engineering, observability, and on-call operations (engineers participate in on-call rotations).

Notable / other: Growing North American business is a strategic priority for 2025–2026. CRO Mark Fieldhouse appointed Jan 2026 to drive commercial expansion. Backed by Visa, Goldman Sachs, and Mastercard as strategic investors — strong signal of institutional confidence. Payment infrastructure is mission-critical, high-reliability domain. Company is a "quiet giant" in UK fintech (TechCrunch, 2024). Currently expanding the Canada engineering team specifically for US Instant Payments go-lives (targeted Autumn 2025 and beyond).

Open relevant roles (sample): Senior Software Developer (Go) - US Instant Payments, Senior Software Engineer (Go), Senior Software Engineer (Go) - Batch Processing
