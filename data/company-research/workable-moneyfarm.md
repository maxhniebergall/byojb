# Moneyfarm — research
Provider/key: workable:moneyfarm | company_type: product

What they do: Moneyfarm is a pan-European digital wealth management platform (robo-advisor). They manage investments on behalf of 130,000+ active investors with over €5B under management. Their platform offers personalized digital investment portfolios, financial planning, and managed ISAs/SIPPs. They recently acquired Willis Owen (UK investment management firm) in December 2024.

How they describe themselves: A "pan-European digital wealth manager" positioned as a modern alternative to traditional wealth management. Engineering culture is described as remote-friendly across Europe; Senior Software Engineer in Test role described as "Europe, Remote friendly." Backend engineering uses Scala/functional programming, microservices, event streaming. Tech is built for a regulated financial services environment.

Size / stage / funding: ~220+ employees across 4 offices (Italy and UK). Raised $208M total; latest Series D round September 2025. AUM grew from £1B (2020) to £4B+ (2025). Based in London (primary) and Italy (Milan, Rome).

Locations / HQ: London, UK (primary HQ). Milan, Italy; Rome, Italy; Cagliari, Italy. NO Canada operations.

Remote policy: Europe-centric remote-friendly policies. Some roles described as "Europe, Remote friendly" or "Milan or fully remote" within Europe. Roles are primarily based in Italy or UK. No Canada or North America presence.

Remote-Canada eligibility: No — company operates only in Europe (UK and Italy). No Canadian offices, no North American operations, no evidence of Canada-eligible remote roles. Backend roles are Italy or Europe-based. Canada is not a supported hiring geography.

Engineering & tech: Backend: Scala (highly preferred), Kotlin, Java 8+, functional programming. Event streaming: Kafka. Container orchestration: Kubernetes. Cloud: AWS. CI/CD: GitLab CI. Microservices architecture. Frontend uses React. Regulated fintech environment with compliance requirements.

Notable / other: Strong Scala/functional programming culture uncommon in the broader market. Recent Series D (2025) and Willis Owen acquisition suggest active growth. Entirely European company — no North American footprint. The "freelance 12 months" and "fixed term 12 months" contract designations on several roles suggest contractor/temporary nature for some positions.

Open relevant roles (sample): Software Engineer - Backend (Italy), Software Engineer II - Backend Platform & Operations, Data Engineer - freelance 12 months, Senior Software Engineer AI - Freelance 12 months
