# Cornelis Networks — research
Provider/key: bamboohr:cornelisnetworks | company_type: product

What they do: Cornelis Networks builds high-performance networking fabric for AI and HPC workloads. Their flagship product, CN5000 Omni-Path, is a high-speed interconnect designed to provide lossless, congestion-free networking for large-scale GPU/AI clusters and supercomputing environments. The company originated as an Intel spin-off in 2020 and competes with InfiniBand and Ethernet in the HPC fabric space.

How they describe themselves: "We build the networks AI and HPC workloads can't wait for — faster, scalable, and above all open." Core focus: delivering "open, intelligent, high-performance networking solutions designed to accelerate the world's most demanding technical computing applications." Values around open standards, customer focus, and high-performance compute.

Size / stage / funding: ~236 employees (as of April 2026). Series B: raised $85.2M total (latest round Nov 2024) from investors including Intel Capital, Global Brain, and Downing Ventures. Founded 2020, Wayne PA.

Locations / HQ: Headquartered in Wayne, Pennsylvania, USA. Teams span multiple US states and reportedly six countries.

Remote policy: Offers a mix of onsite, hybrid, and fully remote roles. Remote jobs are listed on multiple platforms. However, job postings explicitly mark remote positions as "United States Remote" — the "US only" qualifier appears in their listings (e.g., Controller role marked "United States Remote").

Remote-Canada eligibility: No — evidence indicates remote positions require US residency. Job postings on Remote Rocketship list roles specifically as "United States Remote." No Canada-eligible remote postings found. The company does not appear to employ remote workers outside the US.

Engineering & tech: HPC networking hardware and software. Products include CN5000 Omni-Path fabric and Omni-Path 100. Engineering spans ASIC design, firmware, SDK/middleware, NOS (network OS) platform, and Kubernetes platform. Roles listed include Senior Kubernetes Platform Engineer, Senior Software Engineer (AI Middleware, SAI/SDK, Storage Middleware, NOS Platform). Stack involves networking protocols, low-latency interconnects, open standards.

Notable / other: 500+ customer installations globally. Government, academic, and commercial enterprise customers. Planned CN5000 Omni-Path launch in June 2025 as flagship AI networking product. Active competitor to Mellanox/NVIDIA InfiniBand. Intel Capital involvement reflects heritage from Intel Omni-Path program.

Open relevant roles (sample): Senior Kubernetes Platform Engineer, Senior Software Engineer – AI Middleware, Senior Software Engineer - SAI/SDK, Software Engineer – Storage Middleware, Senior Software Engineer NOS Platform Engineer
