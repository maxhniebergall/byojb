# Censys — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:censys | company_type: product
What they do: Censys provides internet intelligence and attack surface management platforms for cybersecurity. They continuously scan the public internet, analyzing hosts and certificates to help security teams identify vulnerabilities, discover shadow IT, and defend their network boundaries.
How they describe themselves: Driven by the mission to "make the internet a safer place" and to be "the one place to understand everything on the internet." They emphasize a culture of transparency, curiosity, continuous learning, and individual impact.
Size / stage / funding: Private venture-backed startup, established growth stage, ~150-250 employees.
Locations / HQ: Ann Arbor, Michigan (HQ), but operates as a distributed company.
Remote policy: Remote-first, supporting employees across the United States and Canada.
Remote-Canada eligibility: Verified yes. They actively hire remote software developers and platform engineers based in Canada.
Engineering & tech: High focus on distributed network scanning, petabyte-scale data pipelines, and fast search indexing. Stack includes Go (Golang) for core services, Python, GCP (Google Cloud Platform), Kubernetes, and large-scale data stores (BigQuery, Elasticsearch).
Notable / other: Holds a prominent reputation in security research; hosts the Censys ARC (Attack Research Center) blog analyzing zero-day threats and scan data.
Open relevant roles (sample): Senior Backend Engineer, Scan Engine; Systems Engineer, Data Pipelines; Platform Engineer.
Sources: censys.com, censys.com/careers, censys.com/blog.
