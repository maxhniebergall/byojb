# Discord — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:discord | company_type: product

What they do: Discord is a voice, video, and text communication platform originally built for gaming communities, now widely used for general interest communities, professional groups, and social interaction. The platform has ~500M registered users and ~150M monthly active users. Revenue comes primarily from Nitro (premium subscriptions), server boosts, and growing advertising/commerce (Consumer Revenue, Ads Measurement & Targeting). Core infrastructure involves real-time messaging at scale, safety/trust systems, database infrastructure, financial platform, and data analytics.

How they describe themselves: Mission: "shape the future of gaming" and build "the perfect place to talk while playing games on any platform." Culture: values diverse perspectives, inclusive environments, employee input, collaborative decision-making, and professional/personal development. Hiring focus areas include Safety Platform, Consumer Revenue, and infrastructure.

Size / stage / funding: ~700+ employees. Private company; last known valuation $15B (2021 peak) — significantly down since. Revenue growing but company reportedly unprofitable as of 2023/24. Backed by Index Ventures, Greenoaks, Baillie Gifford, and others. IPO speculation has been ongoing but no confirmed date.

Locations / HQ: Headquarters in San Francisco, CA. Secondary office in Amsterdam. Most roles require SF Bay Area presence. A small subset of engineering roles are listed as "Remote (U.S.)."

Remote policy: Hybrid/flexible. SF-first for most roles. Limited remote positions available for select engineering roles (Remote - U.S.). Discord can employ full-time staff in Ontario and British Columbia only within Canada. Roles in other Canadian provinces handled through Pebl (an international hiring PEO partner).

Remote-Canada eligibility: PARTIAL — limited. Discord does hire in Canada but only in Ontario and British Columbia (not all provinces). Engineering roles listed on their job board are predominantly SF Bay Area; "Remote (U.S.)" appears on some data/engineering roles but the reviewed listings do not show explicit "Remote - Canada." Canada hires appear to go through Pebl (PEO). BC is in the Mountain/Pacific time zone overlap, which is relevant. Direct confirmation for specific posted roles required.

Engineering & tech: Python, Go, Rust, Elixir (Elixir for some real-time messaging components). PostgreSQL, Cassandra/ScyllaDB (database infrastructure). Kubernetes on GCP. Engineering pillars: Database Infrastructure, Financial Platform, Safety Processing (trust & safety), Consumer Revenue (Nitro/subscriptions), Ads Measurement & Targeting, Data/Analytics engineering.

Notable / other: Discord reversed a planned sale to Microsoft (~$12B) in 2022. Company has been through layoffs (2023: ~17% headcount reduction). Growth has moderated post-pandemic. Advertising push is a strategic pivot. Strong engineering blog with technical depth (e.g., migration from Cassandra to ScyllaDB). Data engineering roles are strong analytically.

Open relevant roles (sample): Data Engineer - Analytics, Principal Software Engineer - Infrastructure, Senior Data Engineer - Analytics, Senior Software Engineer - Database Infrastructure, Senior Software Engineer - Financial Platform
