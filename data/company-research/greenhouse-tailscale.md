# Tailscale — research
Provider/key: greenhouse:tailscale | company_type: product
What they do: Tailscale develops a zero-config, secure overlay network (virtual private network) based on the WireGuard® protocol. It enables devices, servers, and cloud resources to connect directly and securely, bypassing firewall complexities.
How they describe themselves: Dedicated to "building the new Internet" by simplifying complex networking problems. They emphasize a people-first, remote-first culture with an engineering philosophy centered on "small is beautiful" teams (highly capable, high-ownership groups). They value asynchronous collaboration, transparency, security, user-centricity, and a strong commitment to supporting open-source software.
Size / stage / funding: Private venture-backed startup, established growth stage, ~150-250 employees.
Locations / HQ: Toronto, Ontario and New York City (registered locations), but fully distributed.
Remote policy: Fully remote and distributed globally across Canada, US, and UK.
Remote-Canada eligibility: Verified yes. They actively hire and support remote engineering roles across Canada.
Engineering & tech: High emphasis on network protocols, kernel routing, and security. The core daemon and coordination servers are heavily written in Go, alongside Rust, TypeScript, and WireGuard protocol integrations.
Notable / other: Extremely well-regarded technical writing on tailscale.com/blog. They value written communication skills and pragmatic, robust system design.
Open relevant roles (sample): Software Engineer, Systems; Infrastructure Engineer; Security Platform Engineer.
Sources: tailscale.com, tailscale.com/careers, tailscale.com/blog.
