# 1X — research
Provider/key: ashby:1x | company_type: product
What they do: 1X Technologies (formerly Halodi Robotics) is an engineering and AI robotics company that designs and builds general-purpose humanoid robots (such as EVE for industrial tasks and NEO for home assistance).
How they describe themselves: They describe themselves as a team creating general-purpose androids that work alongside people. They emphasize safe robotics, AI development, and advanced physical engineering.
Size / stage / funding: Scale-up venture-backed company. Raised over $126M in funding (as of 2026), backed by the OpenAI Startup Fund, Samsung NEXT, Tiger Global, and EQT Ventures.
Locations / HQ: Palo Alto, California (HQ), with engineering and manufacturing sites in California. Previously rooted in Norway.
Remote policy: Highly on-site. Because their focus involves hardware, mechanical design, physical assembly, and laboratory testing, most technical roles are based on-site in California.
Remote-Canada eligibility: Verified No. 1X Technologies does not maintain payroll, operations, or offices in Canada, and they do not hire remote workers based in Canada.
Engineering & tech: Humanoid robotics design, control theory, reinforcement learning, physical simulation, embedded systems, hardware-in-the-loop testing.
Notable / other: High-profile robotics company backed directly by OpenAI, but highly physical/hardware-centric rather than software/utility SaaS.
Open relevant roles (sample): None for Canada.
