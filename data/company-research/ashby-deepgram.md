# Deepgram — research
Provider/key: ashby:deepgram | company_type: product

What they do: Deepgram is a Voice AI company providing speech-to-text, text-to-speech, and speech intelligence APIs. Their platform processes audio at scale for enterprise customers, enabling real-time transcription, voice agents, and conversational AI. They position themselves as the foundational voice AI layer for builders and enterprises.

How they describe themselves: "Deepgram builds research-driven Voice AI to power the human-to-machine interactions of the future." Core values include being collaborative, curious, and committed to continuous learning. They emphasize "trust, innovation freedom, and customer-first thinking." The engineering culture stresses ownership: "There's a strong sense of ownership within Deepgram Engineering. If you see something that can be improved, you're enabled to go solve it." They maintain ERGs and promote diversity.

Size / stage / funding: ~150 employees. Founded 2015. Series C ($130M, January 2026) at $1.3B valuation. 400+ enterprise customers; 3.3x annual usage growth over four years.

Locations / HQ: Headquarters in San Francisco, CA; secondary office in Ann Arbor, MI. Described as remote-first globally.

Remote policy: Remote-first. "We give Deepgrammers the freedom and flexibility to choose where they work. We hire great talent, no matter where it resides." Annual in-person offsites. Unlimited PTO.

Engineering & tech: Stack includes C++, Python, Rust, JavaScript, TypeScript, React, TensorFlow, PyTorch, Keras. Active-Learning, Engine/Voice Agent, ML Ops Infrastructure, SRE/AI-ML Infrastructure (Kubernetes, AWS, Terraform), Billing & Analytics teams. Strong ML/AI model research focus alongside platform engineering.

Notable / other: 4-day work week perks listed on some job boards. Heavily AI/voice AI product-focused — not general infrastructure. Significant recent funding growth suggests scale-up phase. One of the leading independent voice AI companies (vs. Google, AssemblyAI).
